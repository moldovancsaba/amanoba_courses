## Candidate languages
- Filipino (Tagalog)
- Swahili

## Evidence summary

### Filipino (Tagalog)
- The Philippines has a large connected audience with about 98.0 million internet users and 83.8 percent internet penetration in late 2025, which supports a sizable learner base for online courses. See [DataReportal Digital 2026 Philippines](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2026-philippines).
- The Philippine digital economy is growing fast and AI usage indicators are strong, including wide adoption of AI tools and fast growth in generative AI course enrollments. See [BusinessMirror report](https://businessmirror.com.ph/2025/11/26/phl-digital-economy-to-hit-36b-in-gmv-by-end-25-report/).
- Filipino is not among the top web content languages listed by W3Techs in the top 10 million sites, which implies relatively limited web content share compared to English. See [Languages used on the Internet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_used_on_the_Internet).

### Swahili
- Kenya and Tanzania together represent a large Swahili speaking audience with growing digital access. Kenya had about 23.4 million internet users and 40.5 percent penetration in late 2025, while Tanzania had about 20.6 million users and 29.1 percent penetration in late 2025. See [DataReportal Digital 2026 Kenya](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2026-kenya) and [DataReportal Digital 2026 Tanzania](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2026-tanzania).
- Swahili web content share is very small. W3Techs reports Swahili is used by less than 0.1 percent of websites where the content language is known. See [W3Techs Swahili usage](https://w3techs.com/technologies/details/cl-sw-).
- Low web content share combined with a growing connected audience suggests a content gap and opportunity for localized AI education.

## Recommendation
- Both Filipino and Swahili are strong candidates. Filipino offers higher internet penetration and strong AI usage signals, which can support faster adoption. Swahili offers a larger content gap and multi country reach, but has lower overall internet penetration. A practical sequence is Filipino first, then Swahili if the operational capacity allows.
