# Build a Sport Sales Network in the USA 2026
Amanoba course file: `amanoba_course_sport_sales_network_usa_2026.md`  
Last updated: 2026-02-03  
Language: English

## Course positioning
This course teaches how to design and operate a scalable sales network in sport and fitness across:
- **Segments:** B2C, B2B, B2G
- **Channels:** online and offline
- **Routes-to-market:** direct sales and indirect sales (partners)

Primary market focus is the **United States**, with structured comparison and practical entry points for **EU** and selected **MENA** procurement environments.

## Target audience
- Sales reps and sales leaders in sport, fitness, fan engagement, sponsorship, sports tech, wellness, facilities, and event-related services
- GTM and partnership managers building channel routes-to-market
- Founders and operators selling into clubs, leagues, brands, corporates, and public-sector buyers

## Prerequisites
- Basic sales literacy (pipeline, discovery, proposal)
- Ability to quantify outcomes (ROI, adoption, usage, retention)

## Learning outcomes
By the end, learners can:
1. Build a **sales network blueprint** (segments, ICPs, territories, channels, partners, governance).
2. Run a **3-playbook system** (B2C, B2B, B2G) with clear stage definitions and exit criteria.
3. Launch an **indirect partner program v1** with rules of engagement, deal protection, and enablement.
4. Become **procurement-ready** for US federal discovery and understand EU and MENA tender discovery entry points.
5. Implement a **90-day rollout plan** with measurable KPIs and operating cadence.

## Format and assessment
- 30 lessons, designed for 30 days (one lesson per day)
- Each lesson includes: objective, key concepts, exercise, deliverable, sources
- Recommended assessment:
  - Mid-course checkpoint (Day 15): submit pipeline and qualification pack
  - Final (Day 30): submit complete blueprint and 90-day plan

## Definitions (short)
- **B2C:** selling to individuals (memberships, apps, programs, devices, tickets, experiences)
- **B2B:** selling to organisations (corporate wellness, clubs, franchises, brands, suppliers, sponsors)
- **B2G:** selling to public-sector buyers (schools, municipalities, ministries, public facilities)

# 30-day syllabus

## Day 1 - Sport and fitness value chain map
**Objective:** Identify who pays, who decides, who uses, and who blocks.  
**Key concepts:** buying committee, stakeholder roles, seasonality, operational constraints.  
**Exercise:** map a real offering into: payer, economic buyer, champion, users, procurement, legal.  
**Deliverable:** value-chain map + stakeholder matrix (1 page).  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://hbr.org/2015/03/making-the-consensus-sale
- https://hbr.org/2012/07/the-end-of-solution-sales

## Day 2 - Segment architecture: B2C vs B2B vs B2G
**Objective:** Split the business into distinct motions with different economics and cycles.  
**Key concepts:** segment-specific KPIs, adoption vs contract vs compliance.  
**Exercise:** define 3 motions and write a one-paragraph “definition of success” for each.  
**Deliverable:** segment playbook headers (B2C, B2B, B2G) with success metrics.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://www.salesforce.com/ca/sales/pipeline/stages/
- https://www.salesforce.com/sales/pipeline/management/

## Day 3 - ICP and personas per motion
**Objective:** Build ICP portfolios that match the 3 motions.  
**Key concepts:** firmographics, fit signals, disqualifiers, “non-ICP” list.  
**Exercise:** create 2 ICPs per motion (6 total) with top pains and measurable outcomes.  
**Deliverable:** ICP sheet pack (6 sheets).  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://meddicc.com/meddpicc-sales-methodology-and-process
- https://www.huthwaiteinternational.com/spin-methodology

## Day 4 - Offer packaging and pricing logic
**Objective:** Create packages aligned to decision structure and adoption risk.  
**Key concepts:** pilot vs rollout, tiering, modular scope, SLA/terms.  
**Exercise:** build 3 package tiers for B2B and 2 entry tiers for B2C, plus a B2G compliant bundle.  
**Deliverable:** packaging table + inclusion rules.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://hbr.org/2011/09/selling-is-not-about-relatio
- https://www.huthwaiteinternational.com/blog/spin-selling-questions

## Day 5 - Proof system: outcomes, ROI, and credibility
**Objective:** Define evidence that reduces buyer risk.  
**Key concepts:** metrics, value narrative, case study structure.  
**Exercise:** write 1 case study outline per motion with a measurable outcome statement.  
**Deliverable:** proof pack outline + measurement plan.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://meddicc.com/resources/the-science-of-sales-qualification
- https://hbr.org/2012/07/the-end-of-solution-sales

## Day 6 - Direct vs indirect decision framework
**Objective:** Decide what must be sold directly vs through partners.  
**Key concepts:** partner margin, conflict risk, enablement cost, control vs scale.  
**Exercise:** score 10 accounts: direct, partner-led, or co-sell.  
**Deliverable:** route-to-market decision matrix.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/enroll/csp-overview
- https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=experience.networks_leaddist_dealreg.htm&language=en_US&type=5

## Day 7 - Online channel stack for sport and fitness
**Objective:** Build a channel plan that creates repeatable demand.  
**Key concepts:** inbound, outbound, paid, community, marketplace, referral loops.  
**Exercise:** design a 4-channel plan with weekly activity targets.  
**Deliverable:** channel plan + activity scoreboard.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://www.salesforce.com/sales/pipeline/management/
- https://meddicc.com/resources/meddicc-versus-other-qualification-frameworks-like-bant

## Day 8 - Offline channel stack: venues, events, field execution
**Objective:** Turn events and venues into systematic pipeline.  
**Key concepts:** field rhythms, demo days, partner events, account mapping.  
**Exercise:** plan a 6-week field calendar with clear entry and exit criteria.  
**Deliverable:** offline activation plan + lead capture spec.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://hbr.org/2015/03/making-the-consensus-sale
- https://www.salesforce.com/ca/sales/pipeline/stages/

## Day 9 - Territory and account model
**Objective:** Create coverage that matches buying patterns.  
**Key concepts:** territory types (geo, vertical, size), account lists, whitespace analysis.  
**Exercise:** build a territory model for 3 US regions and define account tiers.  
**Deliverable:** territory map spec + tiering rules.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://www.salesforce.com/sales/pipeline/management/
- https://www.salesforce.com/ca/sales/pipeline/stages/

## Day 10 - Unit economics and channel profitability
**Objective:** Ensure each motion can scale profitably.  
**Key concepts:** CAC/LTV, payback, partner economics, churn drivers.  
**Exercise:** build a simple economics model for B2C, B2B, and B2G.  
**Deliverable:** unit economics sheet + assumptions register.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://meddicc.com/meddpicc-sales-methodology-and-process
- https://www.salesforce.com/sales/pipeline/management/

**Key concepts:** pilot hypotheses, success criteria, rollout plan, SLA, exit terms.  
**Exercise:** draft a 2-page proposal skeleton with optionality and scope control.  
**Deliverable:** proposal skeleton + pricing options table.  
**Sources (URLs):**
- https://hbr.org/2012/07/the-end-of-solution-sales

**Objective:** Close without scope collapse and without hidden risk.

**Exercise:** create a negotiation plan with “give-get” rules and walk-away line.  
**Deliverable:** negotiation plan template + one completed plan.  
**Sources (URLs):**
