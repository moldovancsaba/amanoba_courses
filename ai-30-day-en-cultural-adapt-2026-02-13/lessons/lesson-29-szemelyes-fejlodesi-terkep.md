# Lesson 29: Personal development map

**In one sentence:** Progress is maintained if you have a plan for after the course.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** 12-week development roadmap

## Learning goal

Knowledge retention in a weekly routine alongside work. In this situation, you are a middle manager and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a 12-week development roadmap, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: a 90-day development plan starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **schedule and measure progress after the course**

## Who

**Primary role:** middle manager
**Secondary role:** team member
**Involved decision-maker:** direct manager

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **90-day development plan** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- The plan + measurement together gives momentum. Repetition and reflection preserve knowledge.
- The key is that you always translate the task into a clear output.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the 12-week development roadmap is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- knowledge maintenance in the weekly routine at work in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a 12-week development roadmap gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- you cannot cover the risk of reverting to the old operation with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between middle manager and team member
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where 12 weekly checkpoints and metrics are recorded

## When

### Use it when

- knowledge maintenance You encounter a task in your weekly routine at work, and there is little time
- Based on a 12-week development roadmap, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 12-week development roadmap needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of falling back to the old operation

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a 12-week development roadmap is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable with a 12-week checkpoint and meter fixed with an indicator
- the rework is reduced, because you filter out the error of falling back to the old operation early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of knowledge maintenance in the weekly routine alongside work
- it is more difficult to keep a 12-week checkpoint and a fixed goal

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of the 90-day development plan and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **falling back to old operation**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Provide a short, specific task description so that the format of your 12-week development roadmap is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether the 12-week checkpoint and measuring point have been recorded.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the issue of reverting to the old operation in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, knowledge maintenance in the weekly routine at work.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request is made for knowledge maintenance in the weekly routine at work. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the 12-week checkpoint and meter recorded measurement. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
