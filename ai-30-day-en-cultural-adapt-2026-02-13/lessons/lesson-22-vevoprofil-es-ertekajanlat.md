**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** customer profile + value proposition card

By the end of today's practical task, you will have a customer profile + value proposition card.

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **customer profile and value proposition** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Relevance is the basis of conversion. A targeted message is stronger than a general one.
- Fewer but more precise instructions typically give better results.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the customer profile + value offer card is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- formulating the message of a new product in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a customer profile + value offer card gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if the message is too general, you cannot cover the risk with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between product marketing and sales team
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks where a clear matching of pain point and offer is important

## When

### Use it when

- you are faced with the task of formulating the message of a new product, and you have little time
- based on the customer profile + value proposition card, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- customer profile + value offer card needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not address a very general message problem

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a customer profile + value offer card is created faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with a clear matching of pain points and offers
- rework is reduced, because you filter out too general message errors early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases when formulating the message of a new product
- it is more difficult to keep a clear matching of pain point and offer goal

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of the customer profile and value proposition and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to **too general message** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the customer profile + value proposition card is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether a clear matching of pain point and offer measurement point has been met.


**Don't**
- Don't leave too general a message topic in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need a specific decision for the task of formulating the message of a new product.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for wording the message of a new product. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the measurement of a clear pairing of pain point and offer. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
