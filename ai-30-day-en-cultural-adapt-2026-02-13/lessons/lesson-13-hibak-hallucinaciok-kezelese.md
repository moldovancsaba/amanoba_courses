# Lesson 13: Treatment of errors and hallucinations

**In one sentence:** Only methodical control works against hallucinations.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** source control protocol

## Learning goal

Preparation of a summary containing market data. In this situation, you are a market researcher and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a source control protocol.
Key insight: fact checking starts working when you make the output measurable at the start. At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **Reliably validate AI statements**

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **fact checking** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- A confident tone is not evidence. Evidence is source and verification.
- The key is that you always translate the task into a clear output.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the source control protocol is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- creating a summary containing market data in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a source control protocol gives multiple people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if the risk of passing on incorrect information cannot be covered by human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between market researcher and content manager
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where 5 out of 5 statements are supported by sources

## When

### Use it when

- you are faced with the task of preparing a summary containing market data, and you have little time
- based on a source control protocol, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- source control protocol needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of passing on incorrect information

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a source control protocol is created faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with indicators supported by 5 sources out of 5 statements
- rework is reduced, because you filter out the error of passing on incorrect information early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of preparing a summary containing market data
- it is more difficult to maintain a goal supported by 5 sources out of 5 statements

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of fact-checking and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the risk of **passing on incorrect information**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Provide a short, specific task description so that the source control protocol format is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether 5 out of 5 statements supported by sources have been measured.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of passing on incorrect information in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need to make a summary containing material market data for a specific decision.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general request for creating a summary containing market data. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the measurement supported by 5 of 5 statements. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

- [ ] **source control protocol** output is ready and saved.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the **5 out of 5 statements supported by sources** indicator.
