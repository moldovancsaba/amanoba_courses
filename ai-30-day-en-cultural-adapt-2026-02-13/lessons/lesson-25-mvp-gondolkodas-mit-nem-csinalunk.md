**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** must-have vs later matrix

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **MVP scope** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Focus accelerates market access. The "not now" list is a strategic tool.
- The key is that you always translate the task into a clear output.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the must-have vs later matrix is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- start-up product with limited resources in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when the must-have vs later matrix gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover scope creep risk with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between product developer and developer
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks where clear prioritization of at least 5 elements is important

## When

### Use it when

- a start-up product with limited resources, you are faced with a task and time is short
- based on the must-have vs later matrix, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- must-have vs later matrix needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the scope creep problem

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- must-have vs later matrix is created faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with a clear prioritization indicator of at least 5 elements
- rework is reduced, because you filter out scope creep errors early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in a start-up product situation with limited resources
- it is more difficult to keep a clear prioritization goal of at least 5 elements

### Realistic expectations

- Will improve: practical application of MVP scope and the quality of deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to **scope creep** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the must-have vs later matrix is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether the clear prioritization of at least 5 elements has been met.

**Don't**
- Don't leave scope creep in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, starting product with limited resources for a task.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general request for starting product with limited resources situation. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the clear prioritization of at least 5 elements. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
