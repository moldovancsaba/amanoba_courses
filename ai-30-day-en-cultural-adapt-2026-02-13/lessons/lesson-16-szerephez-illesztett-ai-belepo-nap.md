# Lesson 16: Role-matched AI - entry day **In a sentence:** Now we translate the AI into your own role.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** 5 use case role cards

## Learning goal

Introducing a new team member AI routine. In this situation, you are a customer success manager and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have 5 use case role cards, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: role-specific AI use starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **plan the use of AI relevant to your job**

### Success criteria (observable)
- [ ] The specific daily output is ready: **5 use case role cards**.
- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **At least 4 out of 5 use cases are useful on a daily basis**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step to deal with the main risk: **general use without effect**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** 5 use case role cards
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-routin/nap-16-role-matched-ai-belepo-nap.md`

## Who

**Primary Role:** Customer Success Manager
**Secondary role:** new team member
**Involved decision maker:** customer service manager

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows you in practice how to apply the **role-specific AI usage** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Relevance gives value. Not every tool is good for every task.
- In real work, a quick fix is ​​more important than a perfect first round.
- Quality comes from ensuring that 5 use case role cards is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- introduction of a new team member ai-routine in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when 5 use case role cards give multiple people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of general, ineffective use with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between the customer success manager and the new team member
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where at least 4 out of 5 use cases are considered useful on a daily basis

## When

### Use it when

- you encounter the task of introducing ai-routine for a new team member and time is short
- Based on 5 use case role cards, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 5 use case role cards need to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not address a general problem of ineffective use

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- 5 use case role cards are created faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with useful indicators in at least 4 out of 5 use cases on a daily basis
- the rework is reduced, because you filter out general, ineffective usage errors early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of introducing ai-routine for a new team member
- it is more difficult to keep at least 4 out of 5 use cases a useful goal on a daily basis

### Realistic expectation
- Will improve: practical application of role-specific AI use and quality of deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **general, ineffective use**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 5 use case role cards is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether at least 4 out of 5 use cases useful measurement points have been met on a daily basis.


**Don't**
- Do not leave the topic of general, ineffective use in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision for the task of introducing a new team member ai-routine.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request is made for the introduction of new team member ai-routine situation. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and at least 4 out of 5 use cases useful measurements on a daily basis. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] **5 use case role card** output is ready and saved.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented at least 4 out of 5 use cases that are useful on a daily basis**.

## Guided exercise

### Inputs

- a short description of a real task about the following situation: **Introduction of a new team member's AI routine**
- a short sample of your own that shows what you consider a good role-specific AI usage output
- checklist to rule out the risk of general, ineffective use

### Steps

1. Write in 4 lines the situation, the goal and how you will measure at least 4 daily useful results out of 5 use cases.
2. Create a sample request to generate 5 use case role cards, then request two different versions.
3. Compare the versions in the table and mark which one can go on for real use.

### Expected output format

| Element | What do you type | Expected result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Situation | Introducing a new team member AI routine | Everyone involved understands from one reading |
| Request frame | Target + context + format + constraint | Minimum of 2 usable versions |
| Quality gate | Accuracy + Risk + Next Step | Clear repair decision |
| Final output | 5 Use Case Role Cards | Saved, reusable template |

> **Common mistake:** Many people only look at the risk of general, ineffective use at the end, even though it should already be indicated in the task description.
