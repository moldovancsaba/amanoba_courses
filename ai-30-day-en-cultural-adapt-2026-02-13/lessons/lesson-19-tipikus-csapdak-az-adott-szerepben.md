## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows you in practice how to apply the **prevention of role-specific errors** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Prevention is cheaper than follow-up work. Pattern recognition is key.
- Quality requires two things: a good task description and consistent control.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the error countermeasure matrix is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- in ai-supported work type situations in a dense deadline environment, where a quick and clear decision is required
- when an error countermeasure matrix gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of repeated errors and delays with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between recruiter and hiring manager
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where 5 specific preventive steps count for 5 error types

## When

### Use it when

- you encounter an AI-supported work task in a tight deadline environment, and time is short
- based on an error countermeasure matrix, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- error countermeasure matrix needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of repeated errors and delays

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- error countermeasure matrix is created faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable for 5 error types with 5 concrete preventive step indicators
- rework is reduced, because you filter out repeated errors and delays early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in an AI-supported work situation in a tight deadline environment
- it is more difficult to maintain 5 specific preventive step goals for 5 error types

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of preventing role-specific errors and the quality of the deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **repeated errors and delays**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the error countermeasure matrix format is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether 5 concrete preventive step measurement points for 5 error types have been met.


**Don't**
- Do not leave the topic of repeated errors and delays in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision for an AI-supported work task in a dense deadline environment.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for ai-supported work situation in a dense deadline environment. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and 5 specific preventive step measurements for 5 error types. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
