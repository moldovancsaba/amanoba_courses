# Lesson 24: Pricing basics

**In one sentence:** The price is not a guess, but a value positioning.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** 3 option pricing table

## Learning goal

Service introduction with different packages. In this situation, you are a consultant and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a 3-option pricing table.
Key insight: pricing fundamentals start to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **match pricing options to target groups**

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **fundamentals of pricing** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- A good price is adjusted to the value perceived by the customer, not just to the cost.
- In real work, a quick fix is ​​more important than a perfect first round.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the 3-option pricing table is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- introducing a service with different packages in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a 3-option pricing table gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of underpricing or overpricing with human control

### Connection points

- daily transfers between consultant and financial administrator
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where the target audience and justification are important for all three options

## When

### Use it when

- you are faced with the task of introducing a service with different packages, and you have little time
- Based on a 3-option pricing table, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 3-option pricing table needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not address the problem of under- or over-pricing

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a 3-option pricing table is created faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable for all three options with a target and justification indicator
- rework is reduced, because you filter out underpricing or overpricing errors early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of service introduction with different packages
- it is more difficult to maintain a target and justification for all three options

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of pricing fundamentals and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **under- or over-pricing**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 3-option pricing table is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether the target and justification measurement points for all three options have been met.

**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of underpricing or overpricing in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if a specific decision requires the introduction of a material service with different packages for a task.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for the introduction of a service with different packages. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the target receiver and justification measurement for all three options. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
