# Lesson 8: Documents: task description, outline, summary

**In one sentence:** Several useful outputs can be produced from a long material, if you ask for it well.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** 3-level document package

## Learning goal

Handing over 20 pages of background material to the manager and team. In this situation, you are an operational specialist and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a 3-level document package, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: creating a task description, outline, and summary starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **create multi-purpose output from one source**

- [ ] There is a separate control step for handling the main risk: **loss of substance**.

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **task description, sketch, summary** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- The depth of the document depends on the purpose. The same content provides value in a different form.
- In real work, a quick fix is ​​more important than a perfect first round.
- Quality comes from ensuring that a 3-level document package is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- handing over 20 pages of background material to the leader and team in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a 3-level document package gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of losing the essence with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between operational specialist and project manager
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks where 5 key pieces of information are included in all three versions count

## When

### Use it when

- You are faced with the task of handing over 20 pages of background material to the manager and the team, and time is short
- Based on a 3-level document package, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 3-level document package needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not deal with the problem of loss of essence

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a 3-level document package is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable, 5 key pieces of information are included in all three versions with indicators
- the rework is reduced, because you filter out the loss of essence error early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of handing over 20 pages of background material to the leader and the team
- more difficult to keep 5 key information goals included in all three versions

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of creating a task description, outline, summary and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the **loss of substance** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 3-level document package is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether 5 key information measurement points are included in all three versions.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of losing the point in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, hand over 20 pages of background material to the manager and the team for the task.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request to hand over 20 pages of background material to the manager and team for a situation. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and 5 key information measurements in all three versions. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] **3-level document package** output is ready and saved.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the indicator **5 key information is included in all three versions**.

### Inputs

### Steps

2. Prepare a sample request for the production of a 3-level document package, then request two different versions.
3. Compare the versions in the table and mark which one can go on for real use.

### Expected output format

| Element | What do you type | Expected result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Situation | Handing over 20 pages of background material to the manager and team | Everyone involved understands from one reading |
| Request frame | Target + context + format + constraint | Minimum of 2 usable versions |
