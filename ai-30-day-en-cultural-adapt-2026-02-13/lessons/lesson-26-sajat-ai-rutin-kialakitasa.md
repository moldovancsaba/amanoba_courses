# Lesson 26: Build your own AI routine

**In one sentence:** Routine makes knowledge last.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** weekly AI routine calendar

## Learning goal

Planned daily blocks instead of ad hoc use. In this situation, you are an individual entrepreneur and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a weekly AI routine calendar.
Key insight: the daily AI routine starts working when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **create a sustainable AI practice rhythm**

- [ ] The specific daily output is ready: **weekly AI routine calendar**.
- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **5 specific time bands and task types are attached**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step to deal with the main risk: **split use and relapse**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** weekly AI routine calendar
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-rutin/nap-26-sajat-ai-rutin-kialakitasa.md`

## Who

**Primary role:** sole proprietor
**Secondary Role:** Assistant
**Affected decision maker:** yourself

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **daily AI routine** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Regular small steps are more durable than the occasional big sprint.
- Fewer but more precise instructions typically give better results.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the weekly AI routine calendar is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- instead of ad hoc use, planned daily blocks in situations where a quick and clear decision is needed
- when a weekly AI routine calendar gives multiple people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of disintegrating use and relapse with human control

### Connection points

- daily transfers between sole proprietor and assistant
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where 5 specific time slots and task types are fixed and count

## When

### Use it when

- instead of ad hoc use, you encounter planned daily blocks of tasks, and time is short
- based on a weekly AI routine calendar, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- weekly AI routine calendar needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle disintegrating usage and dropout problem

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a weekly AI routine calendar is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with 5 specific time bands and task types recorded
- rework is reduced, because you filter out disintegrating use and fallback errors early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of planned daily blocks instead of ad hoc use
- it is more difficult to keep a fixed goal of 5 specific time slots and task types

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of daily AI routines and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the risk of **disintegrating use and relapse**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the weekly AI routine calendar format is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether 5 specific time bands and task type recording measurement points have been met.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of disintegrating usage and decline in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision instead of ad hoc use for planned daily blocks.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general request is started for planned daily blocks situation instead of ad hoc use. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and 5 specific time bands and task type recorded measurements. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] You have completed and saved **weekly AI routine calendar** output.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the **5 specific time slots and task types recorded** indicator.
