# Lesson 6: Email in minutes - in a professional voice

**In one sentence:** A professional email can be accurate and humane even in 3 minutes.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** Email template that can be used for 3 situations

## Learning goal

Complaint handling, status reporting and follow-up in one day. In this situation, you are a customer relationship manager and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have an email template that can be used for 3 situations, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: writing business emails starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **write consistent responses to different email situations**

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **business email writing** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- The purpose of the email is to elicit a decision or response. Brevity + a clear request gives results.
- Fewer but more precise instructions typically give better results.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the email template can be used for 3 situations and is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- complaint handling, status reporting and follow-up in one-day situations when a quick and clear decision is needed
- when an email template that can be used for 3 situations gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if the message is too long or uncertain, you cannot cover the risk with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between customer relations manager and sales support staff
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks where a clear CTA is included in all three emails counts

## When

### Use it when

- complaint handling, status reporting and follow-up, one day you will encounter a task, and time is short
- Based on an email template that can be used for 3 situations, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- Email template that can be used for 3 situations needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of too long or uncertain messages

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- email templates for 3 situations can be created faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable, all three emails have a clear CTA with an indicator
- rework is reduced, because you filter out too long or uncertain message errors early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings in complaint handling, status reporting and follow-up in a one-day situation increases
- it is more difficult to keep a clear CTA goal in all three emails

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of business email writing and the quality of the deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to **too long or uncertain message** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the email template that can be used for 3 situations is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether all three emails have a clear CTA measurement point.


**Don't**
- Do not leave too long or uncertain message topics in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, complaint handling, status report and follow-up for a task in one day.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for complaint handling, status report and follow-up is started for a situation in one day. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and a clear CTA in all three emails. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
