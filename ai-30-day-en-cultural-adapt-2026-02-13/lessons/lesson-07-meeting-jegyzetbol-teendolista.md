# Lesson 7: To-do list from meeting notes

**In one sentence:** The value of the meeting depends on the quality of the follow-up.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** meeting action log with responsible person and deadline

## Learning goal

Assignment of tasks after a 45-minute status meeting. In this situation, you are the team leader and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a meeting action log with a person responsible and a deadline, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: an action plan from a meeting starts to work when you make the output measurable at the start. At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **create a clear task list from notes**

### Success criteria (observable)
- [ ] The specific daily output is ready: **meeting action log with responsible person and deadline**.
- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **every task has a person responsible and a date**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step for managing the main risk: **tasks without responsibility**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** meeting action log with responsible person and deadline
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-rutin/nap-07-meeting-needtbol-teendolista.md`

## Who

**Primary Role:** Team Leader
**Secondary role:** Project assistant
**Involved decision maker:** head of department

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **action plan from meeting** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- A good summary records the decision and the person responsible, not just a story of events.
- Quality requires two things: a good task description and consistent control.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the meeting action log can be checked and repeated with a person in charge and a deadline.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- Assignment of tasks after a 45-minute status meeting in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when the meeting action log gives a clear next step to several people with a responsible person and a deadline

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of tasks without responsibility with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between team leader and project assistant
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where each task has a responsible person and a date

## When

### Use it when

- You are faced with the task of assigning tasks after a 45-minute status meeting, and time is short
- several actors must act in the same way based on the meeting action log with the person in charge and the deadline

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- meeting action log needs to be rewritten every time with a person in charge and a deadline
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of tasks without responsibility

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a meeting action log with responsible person and deadline is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with a responsible person for each task and a date indicator
- rework is reduced, because you filter out errors of tasks without responsibility early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of assigning tasks after a 45-minute status meeting
- it is more difficult to keep a responsible and date goal for each task

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of the action plan from the meeting and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the risk of **tasks without responsibility**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the meeting action log with the person responsible and the deadline is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether each task has a responsible person and date measurement point.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of unresponsible tasks in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision for the assignment of tasks after a 45-minute status meeting.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** an overly general request is made for the assignment of tasks after a 45-minute status meeting. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the person responsible for each task and the date measurement. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] **meeting action log with responsible person and deadline** output is ready and saved.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the **responsible person and date** indicator for each task.
