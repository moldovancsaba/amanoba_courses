## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **action plan and next steps** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Closing is valuable if it is linked to a specific next step.
- Fewer but more precise instructions typically give better results.
- Quality comes from ensuring that a 30-day AI action plan is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- targeted implementation of the first month after the course in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a 30-day AI action plan gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if knowledge remains, there will be no practice, you cannot cover risk with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between a new student and an accountable partner
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where a task list divided into 30 days counts with a weekly review

## When

### Use it when

- you are faced with a task of targeted implementation of the first month after the course, and there is little time
- Based on a 30-day AI action plan, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 30-day AI action plan needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not manage, knowledge remains, practice will not be a problem

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a 30-day AI action plan is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable with a task list divided into 30 days, with a weekly review and indicator
- rework is reduced, because you filter it early, knowledge remains, practice will not make mistakes

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of targeted implementation of the first month after the course
- it is more difficult to keep a task list divided into 30 days, with a weekly review goal

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of the action plan and next steps and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the risk of **remaining knowledge, no practice**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 30-day AI action plan is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether the 30-day task list has been completed, with a weekly review as a measurement point.


**Don't**
- Don't leave it in the background, knowledge will remain, practice will not be a topic, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need a specific decision for the task of the first month after the course.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for the targeted execution of the first month after the course. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the 30-day task list with a weekly review measurement. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
