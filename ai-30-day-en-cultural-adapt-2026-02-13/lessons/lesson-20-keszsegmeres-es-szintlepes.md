# Lesson 20: Skill measurement and leveling

**In one sentence:** Now we measure where you are on a real task, not just a feeling.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** individual skill evaluation sheet

## Learning goal

Carrying out a mini-project from task description to finished material. In this situation, you are a small business owner and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
At the end of today's practical task, you will have an individual skill evaluation sheet ready to reuse tomorrow.
Key insight: skill measurement starts to work when you make the output measurable at the start. At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **measure the quality of your own AI workflow**

- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **evaluation form filled in 4 dimensions**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step for handling the main risk: **inaccurate self-assessment**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** individual skill evaluation sheet
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-rutin/nap-20-keszsegmeres-es-zintlepes.md`

## Who

**Primary role:** small business owner
**Secondary role:** virtual assistant
**Involved decision maker:** customer

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **skill measurement** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- Development requires measurement. It's hard to improve what you don't measure.
- In real work, a quick fix is ​​more important than a perfect first round.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the individual skill evaluation sheet is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- carrying out a mini-project from task description to finished material in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when an individual skill evaluation sheet gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of inaccurate self-assessment with human control

### Connection points

- daily transfers between the small business owner and the virtual assistant
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where the evaluation sheet is filled in 4 dimensions and counts

## When

### Use it when

- carrying out a mini-project from task description to finished material, you encounter a task, and time is short
- based on an individual skill evaluation sheet, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- individual skill evaluation sheet needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not address the problem of inaccurate self-assessment

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- an individual skill evaluation sheet is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality evaluation sheet will be more measurable if it is filled in with indicators in 4 dimensions
- rework is reduced, because you filter out inaccurate self-assessment errors early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of completing a mini project from task description to finished material
- it is more difficult to keep an evaluation sheet filled out in 4 dimensions

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of skill measurement and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to **inaccurate self-assessment** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the individual skill evaluation sheet is clear from the beginning.
- Before finalizing, check whether the evaluation sheet has been completed in 4 dimensions, measuring point.


**Don't**
- Do not leave the topic of inaccurate self-evaluation in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need a specific decision.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request is made for the situation of carrying out a mini project from task description to finished material. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the evaluation sheet filled in in 4 dimensions. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] You have completed and saved the **individual skill evaluation sheet** output.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the **evaluation form completed in 4 dimensions** indicator.

## Guided exercise

### Inputs

- a short description of a real task from the following situation: **Completing a mini-project from task description to finished material**
- a short personal sample that shows what you consider a good skill measurement output
- checklist to exclude the risk of inaccurate self-assessment

### Steps

1. Write in 4 lines the situation, the goal and how you will measure the result by filling in the 4 dimensions of the evaluation sheet.
2. Prepare a request sample for the production of an individual skill evaluation sheet, then request two different versions.
3. Compare the versions in the table and mark which one can go on for real use.

### Expected output format

| Element | What do you type | Expected result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Situation | Carrying out a mini project from task description to finished material | Everyone involved understands from one reading |
| Request frame | Target + context + format + constraint | Minimum of 2 usable versions |
| Quality gate | Accuracy + Risk + Next Step | Clear repair decision |
| Final output | individual skill evaluation sheet | Saved, reusable template |

> **Common mistake:** Many people only look at the risk of inaccurate self-assessment at the end, even though it should already be indicated in the task description.
