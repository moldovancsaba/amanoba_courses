# Lesson 27: 60 second short demo with AI

**In one sentence:** The 60-second short presentation teaches discipline: only the essentials remain.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** 60-second short presentation script

## Learning goal

Presentation to a manager or partner in a short time window. In this situation, you are a business developer and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a short 60-second presentation script.
Key insight: a short presentation structure starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **create a short and convincing value communication**

- [ ] There is a separate control step for handling the main risk: **collapsing message**.

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **short presentation structure** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- A good short presentation follows a problem-solution-value-CTA logic.
- Quality requires two things: a good task description and consistent control.
- Quality comes from ensuring that a 60-second short presentation script is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- introduction to a manager or partner in a short window of time in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a 60-second short presentation script gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of disintegrating messages with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between business developer and project coordinator
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where a short presentation between 60-75 seconds counts

## When

### Use it when

- introduction to a manager or partner, you are faced with a task in a short time window, and time is short
- Based on a short presentation script of 60 seconds, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- A short 60-second presentation script needs to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the disintegrating message problem

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a short presentation script of 60 seconds is prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable with a short presentation lasting between 60-75 seconds
- rework is reduced, because you filter out disintegrating message errors early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases when introducing a manager or partner in a short time window situation
- it is more difficult to keep a short presentation between 60-75 seconds

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of a short presentation structure and the quality of the deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the risk of **message falling apart**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 60-second short presentation script is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether a short presentation has been completed, a measurement point remains between 60-75 seconds.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the message topic falling apart in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Don't ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, a presentation to a manager or partner in a short time window for a task.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for an introduction to a manager or partner in a short time window. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the short presentation between 60-75 seconds of measurement. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
