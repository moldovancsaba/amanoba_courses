# Lesson 21: Idea validation with AI

**In one sentence:** An idea becomes a product only if you validate the assumptions.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** hypothesis and test sheet

## Learning goal

Quick pre-screening of a new service. In this situation, you are the founder of a start-up and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
At the end of today's practical task, you will have a hypothesis and test sheet ready to reuse tomorrow.
Key insight: idea validation starts working when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **decompose an idea into measurable hypotheses**

### Success criteria (observable)
- [ ] The specific daily output is ready: **hypothesis and test sheet**.
- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **at least 3 testable hypotheses**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step for managing the main risk: **treat assumption as fact**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** hypothesis and test sheet
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-rutin/nap-21-otletvalidalas-ai-val.md`

## Who

**Primary role:** founder of a start-up business
**Secondary role:** product developer
**Involved decision maker:** investor

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **idea validation** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- The purpose of validation is to quickly recognize a mistake, not to seek confirmation.
- The key is that you always translate the task into a clear output.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the hypothesis and test sheet is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- quick pre-screening of a new service in situations where a quick and clear decision is required
- when a hypothesis and a test sheet give several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you treat an assumption as a fact, you cannot cover the risk with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between the founder of the start-up and the product developer
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks where at least 3 testable hypotheses matter

## When

### Use it when

- you are faced with the task of quickly screening a new service, and you are short on time
- based on hypothesis and test sheet, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- hypothesis and test sheet need to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of treating assumption as fact

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- hypothesis and test sheets are prepared faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with at least 3 testable hypothesis indicators
- rework is reduced, because you filter out the mistake of treating assumptions as facts early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in the situation of rapid pre-screening of a new service
- it is more difficult to maintain at least 3 testable hypothesis goals

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: the practical application of idea validation and the quality of the deliverable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **treating assumption as fact**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the hypothesis and test sheet is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether at least 3 testable hypothesis measurement points have been met.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of treating assumption as fact in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need a specific decision for the task of quick pre-screening of material for a new service.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for quick pre-screening of a new service. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no relationship between the output and at least 3 testable hypothesis measurements. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] You have completed and saved **hypothesis and test sheet** output.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the indicator **at least 3 testable hypotheses**.
