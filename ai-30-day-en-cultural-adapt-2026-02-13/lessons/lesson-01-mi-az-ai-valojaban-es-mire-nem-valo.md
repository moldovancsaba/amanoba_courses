# Lesson 1: What is AI really - and what is it NOT for?

**In one sentence:** Let's start with the clear: what you can ask AI and what you shouldn't entrust it to.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Tangible output:** AI usage map for 3 tasks

## Learning goal

Speeding up daily customer service tasks with data protection risk. In this situation, you are a customer service coordinator and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have an AI usage map for 3 tasks, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: Clarifying the role of AI starts to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **separate the tasks that are suitable for AI and those that are not**

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows you in practice how to apply the **Clarifying the role of AI** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- AI is a tool, not a decision maker. A good result requires a clear goal and human control.
- The key is that you always translate the task into a clear output.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the AI ​​usage map can be checked and repeated for 3 tasks.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- acceleration of daily customer service tasks in situations with a data protection risk, when a quick and clear decision is required
- when an AI usage map gives a clear next step to several people for 3 tasks

### Do not use this when

- if the risk of accidental entry of sensitive data cannot be covered by human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between the customer service coordinator and the data protection officer
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where a correct risk classification counts for 3/3 tasks

## When

### Use it when

- speeding up daily customer service tasks, you face a task with a data protection risk, and time is short
- AI usage map based on 3 tasks, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- AI usage map needs to be rewritten every time for 3 tasks
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of accidentally entering sensitive data

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- an AI usage map for 3 tasks is created faster, with fewer rounds
- quality will be more measurable with a correct risk classification indicator for 3/3 tasks
- rework is reduced, because you filter out the error of accidentally entering sensitive data early

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases in a situation where daily customer service tasks are accelerated in addition to data protection risks
- it is more difficult to maintain the correct risk classification target for 3/3 tasks

### Realistic expectation
- It will improve: clarifying the role of AI, its practical application and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to **accidental entry of sensitive data** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the AI usage map for 3 tasks is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether the correct risk classification measurement point has been met for 3/3 tasks.


**Don't**
- Do not leave the topic of accidentally entering sensitive data in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision for the task of speeding up daily customer service tasks with data protection risk.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request to speed up daily customer service tasks with a data protection risk. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and the correct risk classification measurement for 3/3 tasks. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
