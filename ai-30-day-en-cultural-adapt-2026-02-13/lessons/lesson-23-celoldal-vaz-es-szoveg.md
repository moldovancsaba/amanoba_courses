## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **landing page structure and copy** method in a real work situation.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- On the landing page, the order is decisive: problem, solution, evidence, action.
- Quality requires two things: a good task description and consistent control.
- Quality comes from ensuring that the landing page draft can be checked and repeated with hero and CTA.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying special attention to the **ambiguous offer** risk. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the landing page sketch with hero and CTA is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether hero, evidence, offer, CTA are all present measurement points.


**Don't**
- Don't leave an unclear offer topic in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, quick campaign launch with minimal pages for the task.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for quick campaign launch with minimal pages. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and measurement hero, proof, offer, CTA are all present. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.
