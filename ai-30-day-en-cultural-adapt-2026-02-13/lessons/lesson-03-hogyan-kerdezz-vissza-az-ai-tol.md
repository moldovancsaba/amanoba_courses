# Lesson 3: How to ask AI back?

**In one sentence:** Asking back is not a mistake, but a professional way of working.
**Time:** 20-30 minutes **Tangible Output:** 5 Question Questionnaire Template

## Learning goal

Creating a to-do list from missing meeting notes. In this situation, you are the project coordinator and you have to make a quick but reliable decision.
By the end of today's practical task, you will have a 5-question feedback template, which you can use again tomorrow.
Key insight: clarifying questions start to work when you make the output measurable right from the start.

At the end of the lesson, you will be able to: **build an executable task from an inaccurate task description**

### Success criteria (observable)
- [ ] The specific daily output is ready: **5-question questionnaire template**.
- [ ] At least one measurable indicator is attached to the output: **at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation**.
- [ ] There is a separate control step for managing the main risk: **work starting in the wrong direction**.

### Output you will create today
- **Name:** 5-question questionnaire template
- **Format:** editable board and short decision note
- **Where saved:** `ai-routine/day-03-how-to-ask-back-to-ai-tol.md`

## Who

**Primary role:** project coordinator
**Secondary Role:** Team Leader
**Involved decision maker:** customer

## What

### What is this?

This lesson shows in practice how to apply the **clarifying questions** method in a real work situation.
The goal is not a theory, but an output that you can really use in your next working day.

### What isn't this?

This is not a generic AI demo, and it is not busywork.

### 2-minute theory

- You reduce uncertainty with questions. The clearer the task description, the less follow-up work.
- Quality requires two things: a good task description and consistent control.
- The quality here is provided by the fact that the 5-question questionnaire template is verifiable and repeatable.

### Key concepts

- **Working framework:** a short guide that describes the three objectives, format and limits.
- **Quality gate:** quick check before accepting the output.

## Where

### It works fine here

- creating a to-do list from incomplete meeting notes in situations where a quick and clear decision is needed
- when a 5-question survey template gives several people a clear next step

### Do not use this when

- if you cannot cover the risk of work going in the wrong direction with human control

### Connection points

- daily handovers between project coordinator and team leader
- short statuses and decision preparation notes
- repetitive tasks, where at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation

## When

### Use it when

- you encounter the task of creating a to-do list from incomplete meeting notes, and you have little time
- Based on a 5-question questioning template, several actors must act in the same way

### Frequency

At least once per workday, and every time for recurring tasks.

### Late warning signs

- 5 question questionnaire templates need to be rewritten every time
- you often get back that the content does not handle the problem of work starting in the wrong direction

## Why it matters

### Practical benefits

- a 5-question questionnaire template is prepared more quickly, with fewer rounds
- the quality will be more measurable, at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation with an indicator
- rework is reduced, because you filter out work errors that start in the wrong direction early on

### What happens if you skip it

- the number of misunderstandings increases when creating a to-do list from incomplete meeting notes
- it is more difficult to keep at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation goal

### Realistic expectations

- It will improve: the practical application of clarifying questions and the quality of the transferable output.
- Not guaranteed: infallible response without human control in every run.

## How

### Step by step

1. Name the work situation and the expected result exactly. 2. Write down the mandatory limits (time frame, scope, style, prohibitions). 3. Request the first AI version in a specific format. 4. Run a 3-point quality gate, paying particular attention to the risk of **work starting in the wrong direction**. 5. Fix the output, then save it as a usable template.

### Do / Don't

**Do**
- Enter a short, specific task description so that the format of the 5-question inquiry template is clear from the start.
- Before finalizing, check whether at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectations measurement point.


**Don't**
- Don't leave the topic of work going in the wrong direction in the background, even if the answer seems good at first.
- Do not ask for a general text if you need material for a specific decision, for the task of making a to-do list from incomplete meeting notes.

### Common errors and their correction

- **Error:** too general a request for creating a to-do list from an incomplete meeting note. **Correction:** name the person concerned, the deadline and the decision target.
- **Error:** there is no connection between the output and at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation. **Fix:** record 1 binary pass/fail result at the end of each run.

### It's ready if

- [ ] You have completed and saved the output of the **5 question questionnaire template**.
- [ ] You checked the output according to at least 3 quality criteria.
- [ ] You have documented the indicator **at least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expectation**.

## Guided exercise

### Inputs

- a short description of a real task about the following situation: **Creating a to-do list from an incomplete meeting note**
- a short sample of your own, which shows what you consider to be a good clarifying question output
- checklist to exclude the risk of work going in the wrong direction

### Steps

1. Write down the situation, the goal and how you will measure it in 4 lines. At least 4 out of 5 questions actually clarify the expected result.
2. Create a sample request to generate a 5-question survey template, then request two different versions.
3. Compare the versions in the table and mark which one can go on for real use.

### Expected output format

| Element | What do you type | Expected result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Situation | Creating a to-do list from missing meeting notes | Everyone involved understands from one reading |
| Request frame | Target + context + format + constraint | Minimum of 2 usable versions |
| Quality gate | Accuracy + Risk + Next Step | Clear repair decision |
| Final output | 5 Question Questionnaire Template | Saved, reusable template |

> **Pro tip:** A 5-question feedback template will be stable if you check it with the same 3 questions after each run.
