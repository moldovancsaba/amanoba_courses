# Lesson 1 Quiz Pool

**Question:** A builder says, "My AI app will use GPT-4 and a dashboard." Which revision makes this a product promise with a concrete outcome?

A) "My AI app will use GPT-4 and provide a sleek dashboard for modern teams."
B) "Freelance designers get a 5 minute brief from messy notes so they can send proposals faster."
C) "The app will include templates, automations, and a chat window for users."
D) "We will add more models and more integrations to cover more use cases."

**Correct:** B

**Why correct:** It states a clear user, the problem context, and the specific outcome delivered.
**Why others are wrong:**
- A: Mentions tech and look and feel, not the user outcome.
- B: This is correct.
- C: Lists features without a user result.
- D: Describes expansion, not a promise.

**Tags:** #promise #difficulty-easy #type-scenario

**Question:** A founder writes, "Teams save time with our agent." Which success check is the best binary criterion for that promise?

A) "Users say the agent feels helpful and smart after trying it."
B) "At least 7 of 10 trials produce a usable draft in under 8 minutes."
C) "The agent completes tasks much faster than before for most users."
D) "Customers are more productive after using the app in general."

**Correct:** B

**Why correct:** It is measurable and can be answered yes or no with a time bound test.
**Why others are wrong:**
- A: Subjective and not binary.
- B: This is correct.
- C: Vague and not measurable.
- D: Too general and not testable.

**Tags:** #success-criteria #difficulty-easy #type-metric

**Question:** You must choose one promise to keep scope tight for a paid AI app. Which promise best reduces scope drift?

C) "A dashboard with AI chat, file uploads, and analytics for all teams."

**Why correct:** It is narrow, specific, and tied to a single user problem and outcome.
**Why others are wrong:**
- A: Too broad and undefined.
- B: This is correct.
- C: Feature list without outcome or focus.
- D: Overly broad with no concrete user.

**Question:** A builder wrote these success checks: "feels fast", "pretty interface", and "users like it". What is the best fix?

A) "Keep them, and add one technical metric like response time."
B) "Replace them with binary checks tied to user outcomes and time limits."
C) "Remove them and focus only on feature completion."
D) "Ask friends if they like the interface and use that as success." 

**Correct:** B

**Why correct:** Success checks must be observable and binary, not subjective opinions.
**Why others are wrong:**
- A: Still subjective and not outcome based.
- B: This is correct.
- C: Features do not prove outcomes.
- D: Feedback without criteria is unreliable.
